Validator Deep Dive

Running a validator — what it actually involves

A Canton validator is not a mining rig or a staking node in the Ethereum sense. It's a participant node that stores your data, signs your transactions, and connects you to the Global Synchronizer. Here's the complete picture.

What a validator does Core functions
Stores your private contract data and makes it available via Ledger API (RPC)
Participates in consensus on your behalf for all transactions you're party to
Connects to the Global Synchronizer every 10-minute round to demonstrate liveness
Mints reward coupons auto-issued to your admin party each round (must be claimed same round)
Pays traffic fees in CC for network usage — priced in USD terms per MB of traffic
Validator reward streams 3 income types
Proof-of-life rewards: Earned every round just for staying online and connected. Binary — on or off.
Transaction rebates: A portion of traffic fees returned when you send CC. Like crypto cashback — lowers your net cost.
App reward share: If you're an app provider, up to 62% of the global CC pool proportional to your transaction volume.
No stake required · No slashing · No fixed APY
Reward rate depends on: # validators, CC price, network activity
How to get approved Step-by-step
Get sponsored by an existing SV, validator, app provider, or Canton Foundation member
Set up DevNet environment (Kubernetes or VM, fixed egress IP, OIDC auth)
Sponsor submits your IP for whitelisting on DevNet to the Super Validators
Progress to TestNet → MainNet via the Featured Apps and Validators Committee
Go live — liveness rewards begin immediately on your first connected round
Super Validator vs Validator Key differences
Super Validators (13 total): Operate the Global Synchronizer. Invitation-only. Currently earn ~20% of rewards, decreasing to ~10% by 2029. Equal governance vote. Examples: Goldman, Digital Asset, DRW, Cumberland, Kiln.
Regular Validators (~600): Validate only their own transactions. Accessible via sponsorship. Earn liveness + transaction rewards.
SV path: Canton Strategic Holdings (NASDAQ: CNTN) raised $545M to become a Super Validator — the institutional interest is real and growing.
Critical: Holding fees
Canton charges continuous holding fees on CC balances. This is the burn side of the burn-mint equilibrium. Your CC balance decreases over time if not offset by rewards or activity. The network is designed to reward active participants who are constantly earning and spending CC, not passive holders. Factor this into any treasury strategy.

App Reward Deep Dive

The 62% pool — how Featured Application rewards work

This is the real opportunity. Until mid-2029, 62% of all Canton Coin minted goes to Featured Application providers — proportional to how many transactions they drive. The pool is ~516M CC/month = $77.4M/month at $0.15. Here's the full mechanics.

How app rewards are calculated The formula
Every 10-minute round, reward coupons are issued proportionally to app transaction volume
Your share = (your tx this round) ÷ (total network tx) × (round's mintable CC)
Max cap: $1.50 per transaction in CC (prevents gaming by one massive app)
Coupons must be redeemed in the next round — unclaimed coupons are lost forever
Formula (monthly):
(Your tx ÷ 40M total tx) × (516M CC × $0.15)
= your monthly USD app rewards
Real examples at $0.15 CC Earnings math
10k tx/mo (small app): (10k ÷ 40M) × $77.4M = $19,350/mo
100k tx/mo (mid app): (100k ÷ 40M) × $77.4M = $193,500/mo
500k tx/mo (large app): (500k ÷ 40M) × $77.4M = $967,500/mo
Top apps (Hashnote USYC, 3Trade, Brale): monthly rewards of 100M–500M CC = $15M–$75M/mo
These are real numbers from publicly available on-chain data. The reward pool is Canton's officially published figure.
How to qualify as a Featured App
A featured application on Canton is a production-ready app that delivers real utility. Apply at canton.network/featured. On DevNet you can self-feature through the wallet UI. Once approved, apply a FeaturedAppActivityMarker to your transactions in Daml — this marks them for reward coupons. The rewards are shared among beneficiaries you define, with weighted allocation summing to 1.0.
